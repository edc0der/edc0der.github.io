Word Counter

Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 24, 2026

The short version: Word Counter processes all text locally on your device. We do not collect, store, or transmit anything you type. The only data that leaves your device relates to crash diagnostics and advertising, both provided by Google and described in full below.

1. Who We Are

Word Counter is developed and maintained by Eduard Moya. If you have any questions about this policy, you can reach us at eduard.moya@gmail.com.

2. Data We Do Not Collect

Word Counter does not collect, store, or transmit:

  • Any text you type or paste into the app
  • Your name, email address, or any account information
  • Your location
  • Photos, contacts, or any other personal files
  • Usage history, session recordings, or analytics events

All text analysis (word count, character count, reading time, etc.) happens entirely on your device and is never sent anywhere.

3. Data Collected by Third-Party Services

Word Counter uses two Google services that may collect certain technical information. We have no direct access to this data — it is governed by Google's own privacy policies.

3a. Firebase Crashlytics (Crash Reporting)

Purpose: To detect and diagnose app crashes so we can fix them in future updates.

What it collects:

  • Crash stack traces and error logs
  • Device model, OS version, and app version
  • A randomly generated installation identifier (not linked to your identity)
  • The state of the app at the time of the crash (no text content is included)

When it runs: Only in production (release) builds. Debug builds do not send crash data.

Provider: Google LLC — Firebase Privacy Policy

3b. Google AdMob (Advertising)

Purpose: To display banner advertisements within the app. Ads help keep Word Counter free. You can remove all ads permanently with a one-time in-app purchase.

What it may collect:

  • Advertising identifier (IDFA on iOS, GAID on Android)
  • IP address (used to determine approximate region for ad relevance)
  • Device type, OS version, and app version
  • Ad interaction data (impressions, taps)

iOS — App Tracking Transparency: On iOS, AdMob will only access your advertising identifier (IDFA) if you explicitly grant permission when prompted. If you decline, you will still see ads, but they will not be personalized.

Provider: Google LLC — Google Privacy Policy · AdMob & Privacy

4. In-App Purchases

Word Counter offers a one-time "Remove Ads" purchase. All payment processing is handled entirely by Apple (App Store) or Google (Google Play). We do not receive or store any payment information.

5. Data Retention

We do not maintain any user database. Crash reports collected by Firebase are retained for 90 days per Google's default policy, after which they are automatically deleted. Ad interaction data is managed by Google per their retention schedule.

6. Your Rights (GDPR / CCPA)

If you are located in the European Union, United Kingdom, California, or another region with applicable privacy laws, you have rights regarding your personal data, including the right to access, correct, or delete it.

Because Word Counter itself collects no personal data, most requests will need to be directed to Google:

  • Google Privacy Controls — manage your ad personalization and data
  • Google Account Data & Privacy

For any questions specific to this app, contact us at eduard.moya@gmail.com and we will respond within 30 days.

7. Children's Privacy

Word Counter is not directed at children under the age of 13 (or 16 in the EU). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through this app, please contact us and we will take appropriate action.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this page periodically. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
